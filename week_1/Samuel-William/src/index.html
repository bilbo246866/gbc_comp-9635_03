Hello World

My Name is William

I am an aspiring front-end developer currently seeking opportunities..

I've been at it now for 21/2 years and have a fairly good understanding of HTML5, CSS3, Javascript, jQuery, Node.js, PHP, Bootstrap.

Experience

The majority of my work experience in the technology sector has involved support roles and I have been employed over the years in a technical support role in various forms, reaching back to the late 1980s / early 90's. I have worked as a technical analyst, network analyst, desktop support specialist, Network Administrator, Server Administrator, Level 2 Service Desk and Telecom Analyst.

In 2015 after a downsizing, I decided it was time to change direction and dive into the world of Web Design and Development. I enjoyed the idea of creating something using technology rather than fixing, or cleaning up someone else's disaster. I enrolled at George Brown College in the Continuing Education department and started my first course called Introduction to Web Design and Development. That was in the summer of 2015 and have not looked back since. I've enjoyed completing all of the projects for my classes, both in Web Design and Web Development.

The trick is to not stop "doing" because even if you take a break for just a couple of months a lot can be forgotten, therefore I constantly need to refresh my knowledge, especially in Javascript and jQuery. As far as solid work experience in Development I have to say that I currently don't have too much. Some of the work I do has been volunteer work and I have volunteer mentored at an outfit called "Ladies learning code" which is part of "Canada Learning Code" and helps to teach code to groups of people all across the country. I did this on the recommendation of one of my instructors and I quite enjoy the helping aspect of the job, and it can be a good refresher as well, you're learning as well as assisting the instructor! Beyond this, there's a few Web pages I have updated for friends and acquaintances in exchange for their endorsement. My main priority right now is to create an online portfolio and showcase some Web pages and Web apps.

I look forward to learning many great things in this advanced Javascript class. Location

I have lived in Toronto for the last 30 years, and own a home in Midtown Toronto. I would be willing to travel if the right opportunity came up, and if working remotely was an option, then I would do it. Since Toronto is a well-known tech hub, with many established companies and startups based here, it would seem logical to begin a job search with Toronto, or the GTA. I understand that Vancouver is a tech hub as well and while the milder winters might attract many job seekers, the high cost of living in Vancouver seems a negative. Don't know much about the job situation in Calgary (I come from Alberta, - a great place to live and work) same with Ottawa - don't know much about it. I definitely would NOT want to live or work in the United States.
